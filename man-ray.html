Man Ray played a major role in Dada and Surrealist movements in America as well as in Europe

Man Ray was responsible for several technical innovations in modern art, filmmaking, and photography.

Man Ray

Man Ray (born Emmanuel Radnitzky; August 27, 1890 – November 18, 1976) was an American visual artist who spent most of his career in Paris. He was a significant contributor to the Dada and Surrealist movements, although his ties to each were informal.

He was best known for his pioneering photography, and he was a renowned fashion and portrait photographer. Man Ray is also noted for his work with photograms, which he called "rayographs" in reference to himself.

“I have been accused of being a joker. But the most successful art to me involves humor.” — Man Ray
